HTML5 Web Design Introduction

I’ve done coding before from an HTML, XHTML, and CSS for Dummies superbook – eight books in one – so I was familiar with coding to begin with when I started  the Skillcrush Tech Blueprint course, but it’s definitely opened my eyes to a whole new world. Written from a standpoint of teaching those who have no coding skills at all – and maybe even no knowledge – there was some stuff in there that I was able to blaze through as I was familiar, at the very least with it; but the new stuff… colour me somewhat amazed at what can be done that couldn’t before. So, lesson one: what HTML is and does, and same thing for CSS. Hello, hypertext markup language and cascading stylesheets, how are you and how can you help me make a website? O so much fun! – At least in my opinion. Thank goodness my creative brain is balanced with enough logic to understand such things as coding. Yes, HTML has tags, like < p > for paragraph, otherwise known as a block of text, and < h1 to h6 > for various sizes of headings… that’s basic stuff for making websites look formatted, kinda like a book with chapter headings and the page text. Old but interesting news – next? Yup, I do realize I can insert images with the < img /> tag, italicize with < i >, bold with < bold >, all that stuff… did I mention thatI was familiar with basic HTML coding? Ah, yes, practice makes perfect. And a reference sheet? Handy! Listmania, images, attributes, links, divs… divs? Hmm, never hear of that one before. And iframes? Sounds interesting. O yes, CSS, the stuff that makes a website pretty. And a cheatsheet for that too… niiice. Web fonts? Yes please! Background colours, font colours…box model? What’s that? Classes and ids? New stuff, yay! Float… yes, familiar with that. Ahhh, now I get to make a website – that’s exciting!

In Search of the Perfect Logo

Names come easily to me, or some names at least. And ideas for logos. However, rarely have I created a logo that the experts, at least, collectively approve of, and for some logos I’ve created, the more iterations I do, the less they like it. Although, I was warned that the group I joined was overly critical, so maybe therein lies the problem. Anyway, when I started out, I went a bit crazy with drop-shadows and gradients, because I thought it looked cool, but I did learn in that group that less is more. So, with that in mind, I present my logo, missing the drop-shadow and gradient, but - thanks to help from one expert - a logo I can be proud of. I should note that, if I’m able to start a business, this is the logo and typeface I will be using, as it shows the kind of business I’d like to run - namely a business geared to helping animal-oriented businesses expand their client base and help owners give their pets the best care they can possibly have.
